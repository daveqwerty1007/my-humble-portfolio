Personalized Anime Recommendation Engine

Designed a hybrid recommender for a streaming partner to reduce mid‑season drop‑off and improve content discovery.

Python SQL scikit‑learn A/B Testing
Timeline
May – Aug 2024
Role
Data Analyst Intern

Problem, Stakeholders & Objectives

  • Context: A streaming platform observed mid‑season viewer drop‑off and under‑exposure of niche titles.
  • Stakeholders: Product (engagement KPIs), Content (catalog discovery), Data (model quality), and Marketing (personalization).
  • Objective: Reduce drop‑off and increase discovery without harming overall watch‑time or CTR.

Approach (High Level, Non‑Confidential)

  1. Signals: Collaborative filtering on implicit interactions (views, completions) combined with demographic and session features.
  2. Models: Matrix factorization baseline for candidate generation; gradient‑boosted ranking model for reranking top‑N.
  3. Validation: Temporal split and offline ranking metrics (NDCG@K, Recall@K); sanity checks on cold‑start segments.
  4. Experimentation: A/B test with user‑level randomization; guardrails on CTR and watch‑time; ramp‑up with sequential monitoring.
  5. Delivery: Recommendations surfaced on home, continue‑watching, and genre pages; evaluation dashboard for weekly read‑outs.

Note: No internal data, code, or identifiers are shared here. Methods are summarized at a conceptual level.

Results & Impact

  • Engagement: −20% mid‑season drop‑off on treated cohorts.
  • Discovery: +25% lift in niche title exposure and adoption.
  • Quality: Stable watch‑time and CTR guardrails during the experiment.
